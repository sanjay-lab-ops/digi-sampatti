Kaveri Online, Bhoomi, CERSAI — these portals don't have public APIs. They require session-based scraping, captcha solving, and format normalization. Each portal returns data in a different format (PDF, HTML table, XML). Building a unified pipeline took significant engineering effort and is not trivially replicable.

Digital Escrow — Technical Deep Dive

Why this is the hardest part to build, and why NoBroker didn't do it

An escrow holds a buyer's funds in trust until predefined conditions are met. In property transactions, these conditions are: documents verified, sale deed signed, and registration confirmed. The escrow releases funds to the seller only then. A refund to buyer happens if any condition fails. A dispute panel arbitrates conflicts.

Why NoBroker didn't build this: Escrow for property requires an RBI Payment Aggregator or PA-Cross-Border license, a nodal bank account, and AML compliance. It is heavily regulated. NoBroker's revenue model is subscriptions and ads — adding escrow would require regulatory overhead that doesn't fit their unit economics. For us, escrow is the core revenue engine: we earn 0.25% of every escrow transaction.

# Python — Escrow State Machine (simplified)
from enum import Enum

class EscrowState(Enum):
    INIT         = "INIT"
    FUNDED       = "FUNDED"
    DOC_VERIFIED = "DOC_VERIFIED"
    BUYER_APPROVED = "BUYER_APPROVED"
    RELEASED     = "RELEASED"
    DISPUTE      = "DISPUTE"
    REFUNDED     = "REFUNDED"

VALID_TRANSITIONS = {
    EscrowState.INIT:           [EscrowState.FUNDED],
    EscrowState.FUNDED:         [EscrowState.DOC_VERIFIED, EscrowState.DISPUTE, EscrowState.REFUNDED],
    EscrowState.DOC_VERIFIED:   [EscrowState.BUYER_APPROVED, EscrowState.DISPUTE, EscrowState.REFUNDED],
    EscrowState.BUYER_APPROVED: [EscrowState.RELEASED, EscrowState.DISPUTE],
    # Terminal states: RELEASED, REFUNDED, DISPUTE (→ arbitration)
}

def transition(deal, new_state, actor):
    current = EscrowState(deal['escrow_state'])
    if new_state not in VALID_TRANSITIONS.get(current, []):
        raise ValueError(f"Invalid: {current} → {new_state}")
    deal['escrow_state'] = new_state.value
    log_audit(deal['id'], current, new_state, actor)  # immutable audit trail
    return deal

Every state transition is logged with actor (buyer/seller/system/arbitrator), timestamp, and cryptographic hash. Fully auditable for RBI compliance.

RBI · Reserve Bank of India

Required to hold escrow funds in a nodal account. Without this, we cannot legally hold buyer's money between payment and release to seller. Application fee + ~12-month timeline. Alternatively, partner with licensed PA (Razorpay/Cashfree) as interim.

Banks / NBFCs

Nodal bank account to hold escrow funds. Banks also become B2B customers — they pay us ₹999/call for property verification API, reducing their legal costs by 60–70%. Win-win partnership.

CERSAI · Central Registry

CERSAI registration data requires authorized access. We apply as a financial information aggregator. Enables real-time mortgage status lookup for any property in India.

MeitY · DigiLocker

Enables Aadhaar-linked document access for sellers. Buyer can verify seller's government-issued identity without physical meeting. MeitY sandbox available; production requires NDA + data audit.

Insurance Regulator · IRDAI
Insurance Referral / Broking

To offer title insurance referrals and earn commission, we need IRDAI Corporate Agent or Web Aggregator license. Alternatively, partner with existing title insurance provider (HDFC Ergo / New India Assurance) as referral partner without license.

Bar Council / Legal
Empanelled Lawyer Network

We do not practice law. Empanelled lawyers are independent practitioners. Platform acts as marketplace. Must clearly disclaim we are a technology platform, not a law firm. Revenue split: ₹350 to lawyer per review, ₹149 platform margin.

DPDP Act · Data Privacy
Data Protection Compliance

India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. Property records + Aadhaar-linked identity = sensitive personal data. DPA notices, consent management, data localization. Compliance needed before onboarding users at scale.

Arbitration Act 1996
Online Dispute Resolution (ODR)

Disputes raised in escrow go to an arbitration panel (not courts). Must comply with Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996. ODR platform registration with NITI Aayog framework. Keeps disputes out of courts = faster resolution for users.
