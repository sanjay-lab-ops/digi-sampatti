====================================================
DIGISAMPATTI — MINISTER MEETING PREPARATION
Tough Questions & Confident Answers
====================================================

Read this BEFORE the meeting. Every question a minister,
their secretary, or a legal advisor in the room will ask.

====================================================
SECTION 1 — QUESTIONS ABOUT ACCURACY & MISTAKES
====================================================

Q: "What if your AI gives a wrong verdict and someone loses money?"

A: Sir, three protections:
   First — we read directly from government portals. Bhoomi, IGRS,
   KAVERI. We do not guess. If the government record is clean, we
   say clean. The source is always your government data.

   Second — we never say "definitely safe." We give SAFE, CAUTION,
   or DO NOT BUY. CAUTION always means "consult a lawyer before
   proceeding." We never ask someone to skip legal advice.

   Third — if our report is wrong, the citizen can dispute it.
   We review manually within 48 hours and issue a correction.
   We carry professional indemnity insurance for report errors.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What if your server goes down during a verification?"

A: The citizen pays only AFTER receiving the complete report.
   If any government portal is unavailable, the app clearly shows:
   "Bhoomi portal unavailable — retry in 2 hours."
   We never show a partial report as complete.
   We never charge for an incomplete check.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What if someone gives a wrong survey number by mistake?"

A: The app shows the property details — location, owner name,
   area — before generating the full paid report. The citizen
   must confirm: "Yes, this is the correct property."
   Only then do we proceed. Two-step confirmation prevents
   wrong survey number errors.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "Government portals themselves sometimes have wrong data —
    what then?"

A: This is a real issue and we are transparent about it.
   If our report shows data from a government portal that
   the citizen believes is incorrect, we guide them to the
   official correction process at the Revenue Department.
   DigiSampatti helps them identify the discrepancy — which
   they would never have found without us. We make errors
   visible, not invisible.

====================================================
SECTION 2 — QUESTIONS ABOUT DATA PRIVACY
====================================================

Q: "You are collecting sensitive property data of Karnataka
    citizens. Is that safe? Where does it go?"

A: Sir, we are DPDP Act 2023 compliant. Three important points:

   1. We do NOT store Aadhaar, PAN, or any identity document.
      Only mobile number (for OTP login) and survey numbers.

   2. All data is stored in India — Google Cloud Mumbai region.
      No data leaves India. No foreign company sees it.

   3. We never sell or share citizen data with anyone.
      Not brokers, not banks, not advertisers. Never.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "Can a dishonest real estate agent use DigiSampatti
    to check properties and plan frauds?"

A: Our system tracks usage patterns. If the same mobile number
   runs checks on hundreds of properties in a short time, the
   account is flagged and reviewed. We are building a broker
   verification module — registered RERA brokers use a
   professional account with audit trail. Suspicious bulk
   usage is immediately investigated.

====================================================
SECTION 3 — QUESTIONS ABOUT GOVERNMENT RELATIONSHIP
====================================================

Q: "Are you authorized to access government portal data?
    This could be a legal issue."

A: We have submitted an official API authorization request
   to the Karnataka government portals. Currently, we access
   publicly available data — the same data any citizen can
   access manually. We are asking for official API access
   to make this faster and more reliable.
   We are operating fully within the law. The API
   authorization will formalize what is already legal.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What if the government builds this feature itself?
    Why do we need a private company?"

A: Sir, the government has already proven it cannot build
   the citizen layer — Bhoomi, KAVERI, RERA exist since years.
   But 90% of citizens still cannot use them for verification.
   The government is excellent at digitizing data.
   DigiSampatti is excellent at making that data understandable.
   These are different skills. Government should focus on
   what it does best — we handle the last mile.

   Also: if government builds it, it becomes a government
   portal — slow to update, no innovation, no competition.
   DigiSampatti is accountable to citizens through market
   forces. If our reports are wrong, citizens stop using us.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What revenue does the government get from this?"

A: Two ways the government benefits financially:

   1. Cleaner property transactions = more accurate stamp duty
      registration values = higher stamp duty revenue.
      Even a 5% improvement in registration accuracy on
      Karnataka's ₹4.2 lakh crore real estate market =
      hundreds of crores in additional stamp duty annually.

   2. Fewer fraudulent transactions = fewer court cases =
      Revenue courts process cases faster = less backlog cost
      to government on judicial infrastructure.

SECTION 5 — QUESTIONS ABOUT COMPETITION
====================================================

Q: "Landeed already does this. What is different?"

A: Landeed downloads documents. DigiSampatti interprets them.

   Landeed gives you the Bhoomi RTC. We tell you what
   the Bhoomi RTC means — is it clean or not?

   Landeed has no Kannada support. No NRI mode.
   No AI verdict. No community fraud database.
   No Karnataka-specific land law knowledge.

   Landeed is a national document download tool.
   DigiSampatti is Karnataka's property intelligence platform.
   These are completely different products.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What about lawyers? Will they complain that you are
    replacing them?"

A: We are not replacing lawyers — we are sending them
   more clients. When DigiSampatti shows a CAUTION verdict,
   we refer that citizen to a verified Karnataka lawyer.
   We create a new pipeline of pre-qualified clients
   for lawyers who would otherwise never hear from
   a farmer in Tumkur. Lawyers are our partners.

SECTION 6 — LANDEED REVENUE — HOW THEY MAKE ₹4.07Cr
====================================================

Q: "On what basis is Landeed making ₹4.07 crore revenue?"

A: Landeed makes money through THREE streams:

   1. DOCUMENT DOWNLOADS — Paid access
      Basic EC / RTC download: Free for first few
      Premium bundle (all docs for one property): ₹299–₹499
      Repeat buyers, NRIs, developers pay per download

   2. B2B API — Banks and developers
      Banks doing home loan verification pay per API call
      Real estate developers verify land before projects
      This is their biggest revenue source at scale

   3. SUBSCRIPTION — Power users
      Real estate agents, developers: ₹999–₹2,999/month
      Unlimited document access for professional use

   WHY ₹4.07Cr is actually small:
   They are a national platform with $16.3M funding.
   ₹4.07Cr ARR = ~₹34L/month. That is 68,000 reports/month
   at ₹50 average. With ₹135Cr raised, they are burning
   cash to grow. Their unit economics are NOT profitable.

   DigiSampatti at the same report volume would make more
   revenue per report (₹99–₹499) and be profitable —
   because we are Karnataka-deep, not national-thin.

   "Landeed gives you the document.
    DigiSampatti tells you what to DO with the document."
   That is why our price per report is higher.

SECTION 7 — TECH-SAVVY MINISTER (FOREIGN RETURNED)
Special questions from a minister who understands technology
and has seen international land registry systems
====================================================

Q: "In the UK, HM Land Registry is fully digital and public.
    In Singapore, all property data is on one government portal.
    Why do we need a private company for this in India?"

A: Sir, you are absolutely right — UK and Singapore are the
   gold standard. But there is a key difference:

   UK HM Land Registry: One portal, one format, English only.
   Singapore SLA: Island nation, 728 sq km, 6M people.

   Karnataka alone has 30 districts, 236 taluks, 6 land
   record systems, 3 court systems, Kannada government
   interfaces, and 6.6 crore people spread across
   192,000 sq km. The complexity is 100x.

   Even in the USA — which has a $16B title insurance industry —
   the government never built the consumer layer. CoreLogic,
   ATTOM, First American — all private companies built on
   top of government data. Same model as DigiSampatti.

   The government excels at digitizing data.
   Private companies excel at making it consumer-friendly.
   The best systems in the world use both together.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What AI model are you using? Is it trained on
    Karnataka-specific data?"

A: We use Claude (Anthropic's AI model) for natural language
   interpretation of legal documents. This is the same AI
   used by Amazon, Salesforce, and major global enterprises.

   On top of this, we have built:
   → Karnataka-specific rule engine: 47 land law rules
     for Karnataka (Land Reform Act, PTCL Act, A/B/E Khata,
     BBMP vs BDA limits, RERA vs IGRS distinctions)
   → Kannada NLP: Processes Kannada text from government
     documents and translates to plain language verdict

   As more Karnataka properties are verified, our own
   Karnataka-specific training dataset builds up —
   making the model increasingly accurate for local patterns.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "Have you considered blockchain for immutable
    property records — like what Dubai and UAE are doing?"

A: Sir, excellent question. Dubai Land Department launched
   blockchain property registration in 2020. Georgia (country)
   put all land titles on blockchain in 2018.

   We have designed DigiSampatti with a blockchain-ready
   architecture. Version 1 (current) uses encrypted
   tamper-proof reports with digital signatures —
   the functional equivalent of what matters: an
   immutable, verifiable record.

   Version 2 (roadmap): If Karnataka government approves,
   we can put DigiSampatti verification records on a
   permissioned blockchain — creating a permanent,
   court-admissible chain of due diligence for every
   Karnataka property transaction.

   This would make Karnataka the first Indian state with
   blockchain-verified property due diligence records.
   A landmark for Digital India.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What is your uptime guarantee? Government systems
    cannot depend on a startup that might go down."

A: We are built on Google Cloud Platform — Mumbai region.
   99.9% uptime SLA (same infrastructure as Gmail, Google Pay).
   Even if our application layer has an issue, the government
   portal data is untouched — it sits on government servers.
   We are a read-only layer on top of government data.
   A DigiSampatti outage means citizens wait an hour —
   government land records are never at risk.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "What about cybersecurity? Can someone hack your system
    and corrupt property records?"

A: DigiSampatti cannot corrupt government records —
   we have read-only access to government portals.
   We cannot write, modify, or delete anything in
   Bhoomi, IGRS, or KAVERI. We are a read-only layer.

   Our own database (user accounts, reports) is:
   → Encrypted AES-256 at rest
   → TLS 1.3 in transit
   → Role-based access (no single employee sees all data)
   → Annual security audit planned (CERT-In empaneled firm)

   Background: Our founder has cybersecurity experience
   from CyberXchange — data security is not an afterthought,
   it is built into the architecture from day one.

─────────────────────────────────────────────────

Q: "How is this different from what NIC (National Informatics
    Centre) or CDAC could build?"

A: NIC and CDAC are excellent at building government
   infrastructure. They built Bhoomi, KAVERI, IGRS.
   They are not designed to build consumer products that
   compete for citizens' attention with Swiggy and Google Maps.

   NIC cannot:
   → Iterate the UI every 2 weeks based on user feedback
   → Build a community fraud reporting system
   → Partner with private banks for B2B API
   → Launch marketing campaigns to farmers in Tumkur
   → Operate on startup speed with full accountability

   DigiSampatti can do all of this. NIC and CDAC should
   keep running the government portals — DigiSampatti
   handles the citizen interface on top.
