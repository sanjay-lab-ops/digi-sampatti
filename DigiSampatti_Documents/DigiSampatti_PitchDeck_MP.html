How We Ensure Information Is 100% Authentic

Every piece of data DigiSampatti shows comes directly from official government sources

The most important question for any citizen-facing platform: how do we know the information is accurate?

1

Source: Official Government Portals Only

DigiSampatti reads directly from the same government portals that Revenue Inspectors, bank officers, and lawyers use — Bhoomi, IGRS/KAVERI, RERA Karnataka, BBMP, eCourts, CERSAI, and the Income Tax Benami Properties portal. We do not create or modify any data. Source of truth is always the Government of India / Government of Karnataka.

2

Camera Document Cross-Verification — Forgery Detection

When a citizen photographs their existing property document, DigiSampatti cross-checks what is written on that document against what the government portal shows for the same survey number. If the document matches the portal — authentic. If there is a mismatch — DigiSampatti flags the discrepancy and advises the citizen to verify at the sub-registrar office. This is a forgery detection capability that protects buyers from altered documents.

3

Timestamped Snapshot — Immutable Record

Every verification creates a digitally signed, timestamped record of exactly what the government portal showed at that exact moment. This is not just a convenience — it is legal-grade proof of due diligence. The citizen keeps this report permanently. It is admissible as evidence that the buyer verified the property status before completing the transaction.

4

Roadmap: Direct Government API Partnership

DigiSampatti's long-term data architecture is a formal API partnership with Karnataka's Revenue Department — the same model used by leading Indian proptech platforms. With direct API access, data retrieval is faster, more reliable, and carries formal government authorization. We have submitted this request and are seeking Parliamentary support to fast-track the API access approval.

DigiSampatti does not create data. It delivers government data to citizens —
the same official data, faster, in the citizen's language, with one clear recommendation.

Aligned with Government's Digital India Vision

DigiSampatti is the citizen layer that makes Digital India's land records mission reach every Indian

The Government of India has launched several landmark programmes to digitize land records and protect homebuyers. DigiSampatti is designed to strengthen each of these programmes — not compete with them.

Digital India Land Records Modernisation Programme (DILRMP)

Government has digitized crores of land records across India. DigiSampatti delivers those records to citizens in usable form — plain language, mobile-first, accessible in Kannada. Every citizen who uses DigiSampatti is engaging with government land records, increasing the utilization of the government's investment in digitization.

PMAY — Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana

PMAY beneficiaries receive housing assistance to purchase or build property. A PMAY beneficiary using DigiSampatti to verify their property before completing the transaction ensures that government housing assistance goes to verified, legally clear properties — protecting public investment and the beneficiary simultaneously.

RERA — Real Estate Regulation and Development Act

RERA mandates builder registration and project compliance. DigiSampatti makes RERA accessible to every buyer — not just those who know the RERA portal exists. When DigiSampatti flags an unregistered project, it enforces RERA compliance from the demand side, supporting the Ministry of Housing's RERA mandate.

SC/ST Land Protection — Constitutional Obligation

Karnataka's PTCL Act protects Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe land from illegal transfer. DigiSampatti flags PTCL restrictions in every property report — ensuring that government's constitutional obligation to protect vulnerable landowners is enforced at every transaction, not just at the sub-registrar's discretion.

DigiSampatti does not create a parallel system. It amplifies what the government has already built —
taking Digital India's land records from government servers to citizens' smartphones.

Privacy, Public Access, and Government Data — Fully Addressed

Three questions every government representative asks — answered completely

Is government data being misused?

All property data in Bhoomi, IGRS, RERA, eCourts, and CERSAI is publicly available — any citizen can access it today on the respective portals without login. DigiSampatti accesses the same public data any citizen can access. We do not access any restricted or private government database.

What DigiSampatti adds: A complete audit trail. Every search is logged against a verified mobile number. Government portals have no audit trail at all — a citizen can check hundreds of properties with zero record. DigiSampatti's audit trail is more protective of data integrity than the current system.

Can someone misuse DigiSampatti to check others' properties?

The same question applies to Bhoomi itself — anyone can check anyone's property today on Bhoomi. It is public data. DigiSampatti does not make property data more accessible than it already is.

DigiSampatti's audit trail flags bulk searches. If a single account searches 100 properties in a day, it is flagged and reviewed. Government portals have no such protection. Our platform is more privacy-protective than the current public portal system.

What happens to citizens' data — stored, shared, or sold?

→ Only mobile number (OTP login) and survey numbers searched are stored
→ No Aadhaar, no PAN, no identity document stored anywhere
→ Data stored in India only — Google Cloud Mumbai. No data leaves India.
→ Citizen data is never sold, shared, or monetized with any third party
→ Verified property reports are stored for 30 days, then permanently deleted
→ The citizen's downloaded report is theirs permanently — DigiSampatti holds no long-term copy

DPDP Act 2023 compliant. Built to exceed minimum compliance requirements, not just meet them.

DigiSampatti handles government's public data responsibly —
