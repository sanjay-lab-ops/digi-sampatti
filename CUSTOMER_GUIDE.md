## How to Check a Property

1. Tap **"Scan Property Document"** on the home screen
2. Point camera at the RTC / sale deed / property card
3. App reads survey number, taluk, district automatically via OCR
4. Tap **Continue** — all 7 portals checked instantly

### Option B — Search Manually
1. Tap **"Search Property"**
2. Select District → Taluk → Hobli → Village
3. Enter Survey Number (e.g. `123/2A`)
4. Tap **Search** — results appear in 60–90 seconds

## What You Get — Explained Simply

### 1. RTC (Record of Rights, Tenancy & Crops)
**What it is:** Official land ownership document from Bhoomi portal  
**What to check:**
- Owner Name — must match seller's name on sale deed
- Land Type — "B-Kharab" or "A-Kharab" = government reserved, cannot be sold
- Extent — total area in acres/guntas

**Red flag:** Owner name on RTC ≠ seller name → possible fraud

---

### 2. EC (Encumbrance Certificate) — Last 30 Years
**What it is:** Complete history of all transactions on this property from Kaveri portal  
**What to check:**
- Any mortgage / loan registered against this property?
- Any sale deed registered by someone other than current seller?
- Any court attachment order?

**Red flag:** Active mortgage = bank has claim on property. Do not buy until loan is cleared.

---

### 3. RERA Registration
**What it is:** Karnataka RERA registration for apartment projects  
**Applies to:** Flats / apartments in a housing project  
**What to check:**
- Is the project RERA registered? (mandatory for projects > 8 units)
- Is the project status "Under Construction" or "Completed"?
- Does the carpet area match what builder claims?

**Red flag:** No RERA registration = builder is operating illegally

---

### 4. eCourt Cases
**What it is:** Any active court case on this property (from National eCourts portal)  
**What to check:**
- Any dispute filed by family member / co-owner?
- Any government land acquisition case?
- Any criminal case related to fraud on this survey number?

**Red flag:** Active case = DO NOT buy until case is resolved

---

### 5. CERSAI (Mortgage / Charge Check)
**What it is:** Central Registry of Securitisation Asset Reconstruction and Security Interest  
**What to check:**
- Is there any home loan / mortgage registered against this property in any bank?

**Red flag:** "Charge registered" = property is pledged with a bank as collateral

---

### 6. Guidance Value (IGR / Stamp Duty Value)
**What it is:** Minimum market value set by Karnataka government for stamp duty calculation  
**What to check:**
- Guidance value per sqft for your area
- Compare with seller's asking price

**How to use in real life:**
- Guidance value = ₹3,500/sqft, seller asks ₹4,200/sqft → fair market price
- Guidance value = ₹3,500/sqft, seller asks ₹1,500/sqft → suspicious undervaluation (black money deal)
- Guidance value is updated by IGR (Inspector General of Registration) every 1–3 years based on actual registrations in that area
- You pay stamp duty on whichever is HIGHER: sale price or guidance value

> **Real-life example:** A 30×40 site (1200 sqft) in Yelahanka with guidance value ₹2,800/sqft  
> Minimum stamp duty base = ₹33.6 lakhs. Even if you agree to buy at ₹28L, stamp duty is on ₹33.6L.

---

### 7. FMB Sketch (Field Measurement Book)
**What it is:** Official government sketch showing exact shape and boundaries of the survey number  
**What to check:**
- Does the actual plot shape match the FMB sketch?
- Are neighboring survey numbers correct?
- Are there any internal roads / water bodies passing through?

**Use this for:** Catching encroachment, boundary disputes, and layout violations

## AI Legal Score — What Does It Mean?

| Score | Color | Meaning |
|-------|-------|---------|
| 80–100 | Green | Safe to proceed. All major checks passed. |
| 50–79 | Yellow | Verify before buying. One or more risks found. |
| 0–49 | Red | High risk. Consult a property advocate before any payment. |

The AI score is calculated from:
- EC transaction history (30 years)
- RTC ownership clarity
- RERA registration status
- Active court cases
- CERSAI mortgage status
- Guidance value vs. sale price ratio

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is this data official / government data?**  
A: Yes. We fetch live data directly from Bhoomi, Kaveri, RERA Karnataka, eCourts, CERSAI, and IGR portals. We do not modify any data.

**Q: How fresh is the data?**  
A: All data is fetched live at the time of your search. EC data reflects registrations up to yesterday. RTC reflects latest mutations.

**Q: Can I use this for agricultural land?**  
A: Yes. RTC, EC, CERSAI, and FMB Sketch work for all land types including agricultural.

**Q: What if survey number is not found?**  
A: Either the survey number is wrong, or the village is in a different taluk. Try the camera scan — it reads the exact format from your document.

**Q: Does this replace an advocate?**  
A: No. DigiSampatti gives you the facts. For legal interpretation and drafting, always use a qualified property advocate. For high-value properties (> ₹50L), we recommend both.

**Q: How often does guidance value change?**  
A: Karnataka IGR updates guidance values every 1–3 years, typically after a survey of actual registration prices in each area. After a development (new road, metro station), guidance values often jump 20–40% in the next revision.
